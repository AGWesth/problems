## Solve for x over the real numbers: $| \frac{9x}{10} + \frac{15}{16} |  = \frac{2}{3}$

**Eliminate the absolute value.**

Split the equation into two possible cases:  
$$\frac{9x}{10} + \frac{15}{16} = \frac{2}{3}$$  
or  
$$\frac{9x}{10} + \frac{15}{16} = -\frac{2}{3}$$  

**Look at the first equation: Isolate terms with x to the left-hand side.**

Subtract $\frac{15}{16}$ from both sides:  
$$\frac{9x}{10} = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{15}{16}$$  

**Solve for x.**

Multiply both sides by $\frac{10}{9}$:  
$$x = \frac{-65}{216}$$  

**Look at the second equation: Isolate terms with x to the left-hand side.**

Subtract $\frac{15}{16}$ from both sides:  
$$\frac{9x}{10} = -\frac{2}{3} - \frac{15}{16}$$  

**Solve for x.**

Multiply both sides by $\frac{10}{9}$:  
$$x = \frac{-385}{216}$$  

**Answer:**  
$$x = \frac{-65}{216} \text{ or } x = \frac{-385}{216}$$

## Factor the following: $5m^5 + 25m^4 - 85m^3 - 425m^2 + 80m + 400$

**Factor out the greatest common divisor of the coefficients.**

The greatest common divisor of the coefficients of $5m^5 + 25m^4 - 85m^3 - 425m^2 + 80m + 400$ is 5. Factor 5 out:

$$
5(m^5 + 5m^4 - 17m^3 - 85m^2 + 16m + 80)
$$

**Factor pairs of terms in $m^5 + 5m^4 - 17m^3 - 85m^2 + 16m + 80$ by grouping.**

Group the terms as follows:

$$
m^5 + 5m^4 - 17m^3 - 85m^2 + 16m + 80 = (m^5 + 5m^4) - (17m^3 + 85m^2) + (16m + 80)
$$

Factor each group:

$$
= m^4(m + 5) - 17m^2(m + 5) + 16(m + 5)
$$

Factor out the common term $(m + 5)$:

$$
= (m + 5)(m^4 - 17m^2 + 16)
$$

**Express $m^4 - 17m^2 + 16$ as a quadratic in $m^2$.**

Let $x = m^2$. Then $m^4 - 17m^2 + 16 = x^2 - 17x + 16$. 

**Factor $x^2 - 17x + 16$.**

The factors of 16 that sum to $-17$ are $-1$ and $-16$. So:

$$
x^2 - 17x + 16 = (x - 1)(x - 16)
$$

Substitute back $x = m^2$:

$$
m^4 - 17m^2 + 16 = (m^2 - 1)(m^2 - 16)
$$

The expression becomes:

$$
5(m + 5)(m^2 - 1)(m^2 - 16)
$$

**Factor $m^2 - 1$ and $m^2 - 16$ as differences of squares.**

For $m^2 - 1$:

$$
m^2 - 1 = (m - 1)(m + 1)
$$

For $m^2 - 16$:

$$
m^2 - 16 = (m - 4)(m + 4)
$$

Substitute back:

$$
5(m + 5)(m - 1)(m + 1)(m - 4)(m + 4)
$$

**Final Answer:**

$$
5((m - 1)(m + 1))(m + 5)(m - 4)(m + 4)
